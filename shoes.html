GENERAL PLANT CARE

Though most of the plants available here is low-maintenance, some love and attention is key to make sure that your plant lives a long, happy, and healthy life. It’s also important to note that just like you, plants experience stress too, and it is quite normal for them to experience this as they adjust to their new homes.

Soil

On average, indoor plants are watered once a week. But the best way is to check the soil by dipping your finger or using a stick. If the soil is still soggy or sticky, no need to water yet. Eventually, you will get the hang of your plant's watering schedule.

Water

Water all the way through the pot until you see some seeping out from the drainage holes. This makes sure that the water reaches the plant's root system.

Sunlight

Plants should have a source of light (though the intensity varies). Plants placed along dark corners of the house or in the middle of offices should be rotated to areas near windows so that it can absorb some form of light.

Leaves

Dust may build up on the leaves. To bring bacj the shine of the leaves and to allow them to breathe, regularly wipe them with a damp soft cloth or if your space allows. give them a shower.
